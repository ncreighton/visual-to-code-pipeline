# Niche-Specific Design Guides

Visual design rules, inspiration sources, and anti-patterns by site type.

## Spiritual / Witchcraft Sites

### Sites: WitchcraftForBeginners.com

### Visual DNA
- **Atmosphere**: Candlelit study, intimate, wise
- **Palette**: Deep purples, golds, midnight blues, near-black backgrounds
- **Typography**: Serif fonts (Cinzel, Lora) - timeless, mystical
- **Textures**: Aged paper, starry backgrounds, subtle particle effects
- **Imagery**: Moons, botanicals, candles, sacred geometry, nature

### Reference Sites for Inspiration
| Site | Why It's Good | What to Extract |
|------|---------------|-----------------|
| thehoodwitch.com | Modern witch aesthetic, clean | Dark theme execution |
| witchipedia.com | Reference-style, organized | Content structure |
| learnreligions.com/paganism | Educational spiritual | Authority tone |
| moongiant.com | Moon tracking, clean data viz | Astronomical elegance |

### Critical Anti-Patterns - NEVER USE
- ❌ **Halloween imagery** - No skulls, no spooky, no purple-orange
- ❌ **Centered text layouts** - Always left or asymmetric alignment
- ❌ **Sans-serif fonts** - Serif fonts only for this niche
- ❌ **Purple-to-blue gradients** - Screams generic AI
- ❌ **Bright, saturated colors** - Keep it dark and subtle
- ❌ **Stock photo witches** - No costumes, no props
- ❌ **Pentagram clichés** - Unless contextually appropriate
- ❌ **Glitter or sparkle effects** - Too juvenile
- ❌ **"Learn More" CTAs** - Use mystical language

### Approved Design Patterns
- ✓ Left-aligned hero content emerging from darkness
- ✓ Gold accent elements sparingly used
- ✓ Moon phase iconography
- ✓ Subtle glow effects on text
- ✓ Staggered fade-in animations
- ✓ Dark cards with gold borders on hover
- ✓ Serif typography throughout

## Universal Anti-Patterns (All Sites)

These should NEVER appear on ANY site:

1. **Purple-to-blue gradients** - The hallmark of AI slop
2. **Roboto, Inter, or Arial as primary font** - Default = generic
3. **Centered everything** - Creates visual monotony
4. **"Learn More" CTAs** - Lazy, unconvincing
5. **Generic stock photos with filters** - Immediately recognizable
6. **Excessive drop shadows** - Feels dated
7. **Rainbow gradients** - Unprofessional
8. **Symmetry for symmetry's sake** - Boring
9. **Template-obvious designs** - If you've seen it before, don't use it
10. **Multiple font sizes/colors per section** - Visual noise

## Design Decision Framework

When designing for any site:

1. **Check the niche guide above** - Know the rules
2. **Reference the anti-patterns** - Know what to avoid
3. **Pull from approved patterns** - Build on what works
4. **Generate tokens first** - Establish the system
5. **Create v0 prompts with specifics** - Include anti-patterns in "MUST NOT"
6. **Validate against brand voice** - Does it feel right?
7. **Test against anti-pattern checklist** - Final QA
